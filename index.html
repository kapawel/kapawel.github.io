how Roger improves your game

High-level tennis athletes face opponents with aggressive playing styles. To develop an athlete’s consistency, coaches often use tennis ball launching machines. The drawback with current machines is that they cannot vary the ball spin, speed and placement between launches at a high enough level to challenge the athlete. Our ball machine, Roger, eases the transfer of skill from practice to competitive matches by simulating realistic aggressive playing styles. Roger is configurable to make shot patterns that mirror a specific opponent’s style of play giving an athlete the upper hand during a match.

  • 10-100 mph in 2 seconds speed control
  • 0-2000 rpm top- under- and side-spin control
  • Full-court ball delivery with 2 ft of accuracy
  • 100-ball dispenser compartment
  • 40 minutes of continuous play
  • Easy to store and transport

The next software update enables Roger to link-up with other Roger ball machines for launching the next ball from where the previous is returned to on the tennis court.

Roger 1.0

Roger 1.0 is the first version of the dynamic tennis ball machine developed to demonstrate the requirements listed above. It is composed of a wooden frame with aluminum panels for added rigidity.

inside the machine

Inside the tennis ball machine are three 1KW BLDC motors each regulating the spin of one wheel. A 22,000mah Li-Po battery can safely supply the 180A of current required to operate the machine at its limits: 10 and 100mph alternating ball launches every 2 seconds. Two stepper motors control the platform's pitch and yaw for full-court ball delivery; one stepper motor controls the ball dispensing system. efficient design

The tennis ball machine can easily be reconfigured to reduce its size by two (1.0 x 0.5 x 1.5 meters) for compact storage. The machine can then be placed inside a minivan or pickup truck for easy transportation. The four 3-inch caster wheels enable smooth transportation all the way to the tennis courts.
